How Our Roofing Estimator Works: Simple Steps to Precision

Using the Roofing Estimator is straightforward and designed for maximum accuracy:

  1. Define Roof Dimensions: Input the length and width of your roof in meters.
  2. Enter Roof Pitch: This is a critical input. Provide the rise in centimeters per 100cm run (e.g., a 30cm rise over a 100cm run). This helps the tool calculate the true sloped area of your roof.
  3. Input Material Coverage Rates:
    • Shingle Coverage: Check your shingle packaging for its coverage rate (e.g., square meters per bundle) and enter it.
    • Underlayment Coverage: Similarly, input the underlayment's coverage rate (e.g., square meters per roll).
  4. Add Waste Factor: A waste factor (typically 10-15%) is crucial to account for cuts, errors, and potential damage. Our tool defaults to 10%, but you can adjust it based on the complexity of your roof and material type.
  5. Get Your Estimates: Click 'Calculate Roofing Materials', and the tool will instantly provide you with the estimated number of shingle bundles and underlayment rolls required.

Expert Tips for a Durable and Long-Lasting Roof Installation

Beyond accurate estimation, here are some professional tips to ensure your roofing project is a success:

  • Safety First: Always prioritize safety when working on a roof. Use appropriate fall protection, stable ladders, and ensure good footing.
  • Decking Inspection: Before installing new materials, thoroughly inspect the roof decking for any damage, rot, or soft spots. Repair or replace as needed.
  • Proper Ventilation: Adequate attic ventilation is crucial for roof longevity. It helps regulate temperature and moisture, preventing premature aging of shingles and reducing energy costs.
  • Underlayment Installation: Install underlayment correctly, overlapping according to manufacturer specifications, to provide an extra layer of protection against moisture.
  • Starter Shingles: Always use starter shingles along eaves and rakes to provide proper sealing and prevent wind uplift.
  • Flashing: Pay meticulous attention to flashing details around chimneys, skylights, vents, and valleys. Proper flashing is essential for preventing leaks.
