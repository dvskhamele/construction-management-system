How Our Brick and Mortar Estimator Works: Simple Steps to Precision

Using the Brick and Mortar Estimator is straightforward and designed for maximum accuracy:

  1. Define Wall Dimensions: Input the length and height of the wall you plan to build in meters.
  2. Enter Brick/Block Details: Provide the length and height of your individual bricks or blocks in centimeters.
  3. Specify Mortar Joint Thickness: Enter the desired thickness of your mortar joints in millimeters (typically 10mm).
  4. Add Waste Factor: A waste factor (typically 5-10%) is crucial to account for cuts, breakages, and potential errors. Our tool defaults to 5%, but you can adjust it based on the complexity of your masonry work.
  5. Get Your Estimates: Click 'Calculate Materials', and the tool will instantly provide you with the estimated number of bricks/blocks and the total volume of mortar needed in cubic meters.

Expert Tips for Quality Masonry Construction

Beyond accurate estimation, here are some professional tips to ensure your masonry project is a success:

  • Foundation First: Ensure your foundation is level, strong, and properly cured before starting masonry work.
  • Mix Mortar Correctly: Follow manufacturer's instructions for mixing mortar to achieve the correct consistency and strength. Too wet or too dry can compromise the bond.
  • Maintain Consistent Joints: Use string lines and levels to ensure consistent mortar joint thickness and a straight, plumb wall.
  • Tooling Joints: Tooling mortar joints not only improves the aesthetic appearance but also compacts the mortar, making it more weather-resistant.
  • Curing: Protect freshly laid masonry from rapid drying, extreme heat, or freezing temperatures to allow the mortar to cure properly and achieve its full strength.
  • Cleanliness: Clean excess mortar from the face of bricks/blocks as you go to prevent staining and difficult cleanup later.
