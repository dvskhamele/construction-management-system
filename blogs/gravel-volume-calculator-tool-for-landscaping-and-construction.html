Beyond Calculation: Practical Tips for Working with Gravel and Aggregate

Beyond simply calculating the volume, several factors influence your gravel project's success and longevity:

  • Material Type: Choose the right type of gravel or aggregate for your project. Different sizes and compositions are suitable for drainage, decorative purposes, or load-bearing bases.
  • Subgrade Preparation: Ensure the subgrade is properly prepared, compacted, and has adequate drainage. A stable base beneath the gravel is crucial.
  • Compaction: For load-bearing applications like driveways, proper compaction of the gravel layers is essential to prevent settling and ensure durability. Use a plate compactor for best results.
  • Edging: Consider installing edging (e.g., plastic, metal, or stone) to contain the gravel and maintain the shape of your driveway or path.
  • Weed Barrier: For decorative gravel or paths, a landscape fabric or weed barrier beneath the gravel can help prevent weed growth and keep the aggregate clean.
  • Drainage: Ensure proper drainage is considered, especially for driveways and large areas, to prevent water pooling and erosion.
